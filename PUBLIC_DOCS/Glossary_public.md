# Glossary — Public Terms

This glossary provides concise definitions of key terms used in the Algora Humani project.
It is intended to support clear understanding for public readers and does not introduce new norms.

---

## Algora Humani
A constitutional and societal project exploring alternative models of social organization based on human dignity, equity, merit, transparency, and knowledge.

---

## Constitution
A normative and conceptual framework that defines principles, rights, responsibilities, and institutional structure.
In Algora Humani, the Constitution is a reference for study, demonstration, and refinement—not a decree imposed on others.

---

## Project of Intention
A project designed to be built through deliberate purpose, responsible participation, and practical demonstration, rather than through abstract vision alone.

---

## Demonstration
The process of showing, through practice and participation, that alternative forms of organization are possible and coherent.

---

## Human Dignity
The inherent worth of every person, recognized as non-negotiable and foundational to all constitutional principles.

---

## Equity
Fairness based on real conditions and contribution, distinct from arbitrary privilege or inherited advantage.

---

## Merit
Recognition derived from ethical conduct, responsibility, and actual contribution to the common good.

---

## Common Good
The collective well-being of society, understood as a shared responsibility rather than the sum of individual interests.

---

## Transparency
The principle that public structures, decisions, and processes must be open to scrutiny and understanding.

---

## Technology (Instrumental Role)
Technology is recognized as a tool in service of human beings, operating under exclusive human control and without autonomous authority.

---

## Artificial Intelligence (AI)
A technological system used to assist understanding, organization, and access to information.
AI does not possess agency, authority, or normative power.

---

## LYRA
An artificial intelligence assistant developed within the Algora Humani project to support navigation of documentation, structured explanation, and informed discussion.

---

## Normative Text
A document that defines binding principles, rights, or structural rules within the project framework.

---

## Explanatory Text
A document that clarifies meaning and intent without creating new norms.

---

## Non-Normative / Exploratory Text
A document that examines possibilities, implications, or open questions without prescriptive force.

---

## Participation
Voluntary and responsible engagement with the project through study, dialogue, critique, and practical contribution.

---

## Living Framework
A system designed to evolve through documented reasoning, evaluation, and refinement rather than remaining static.

---

*This glossary is provided to facilitate accurate reading and understanding of the public documents of Algora Humani.*
